Mutton Biryani

Mutton Biryani is a classic and beloved dish in South Asian cuisine, known for its rich flavors and aromatic spices. This dish combines tender, marinated mutton with fragrant basmati rice, cooked together with a variety of spices, making it a favorite at celebrations and family gatherings.

Ingredients:

For the Mutton Marinade:

  • 1 kg mutton, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Biryani:

  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 6 cups water
  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 5-6 cloves
  • 3-4 green cardamoms
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Fresh mint and coriander leaves for garnishing
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt. Allow it to marinate for at least 3 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes.
  3. In a large pot, heat oil or ghee and fry the onions until they are golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions for garnishing later.
  4. Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaves to the remaining onions in the pot. Saut√© for a minute.
  5. Add the marinated mutton and cook until it's browned and nearly done.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.
  7. In another pot, boil water and cook the rice until it's about 70% done. Drain the water.
  8. Layer the cooked mutton and the par-cooked rice in the pot. Sprinkle the fried onions, mint, coriander leaves, and saffron milk (if using) on top.
  9. Cover and simmer on low heat for 20-30 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors have melded together.
  10. Garnish with the reserved fried onions and serve hot.
